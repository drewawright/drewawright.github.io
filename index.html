About Me

Depending on the day, I’m a French Horn-playing .NET developer, or a .NET developing-french horn player. I’m currently taking part in Eleven Fifty’s 12-week .NET program to build my skills.

I enjoy the fast-paced learning environment and need for problem-solving skills that the program brings. Each different skill set, like HTML, CSS, or C# requires its own approach and provides its own challenges.

Prior to ElevenFifty, I worked as a musician, both as a performer and teacher. As a teacher, I worked both one-on-one, teaching private French Horn lessons, and in group settings as a marching band instructor.

I worked to coordinate the desires of students with the educational goals of their band directors and my own goals as a teacher. This balancing of fun and learning, while also meeting the requirements of a third party is a skill I can bring to any work environment.

Working as a musician has helped me develop a keen eye (and ear) for detail, as well as knowing how to transfer technical skills into an artistic expression. Through my horn-playing career, I’ve worked in groups as small as 3 and as large as 80. I am comfortable in both small groups, working as a collaborator, and large groups, shaping my own skills and vision to match what is asked for by a leader.
